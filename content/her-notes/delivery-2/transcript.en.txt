Induction Diary, Part 2.

Although the 39-week cervical exam suggested that I might not need too many interventions, the on-duty OB I met after admission still recommended going through the full protocol to shorten the first stage. What follows is a timeline — the later entries may not be perfectly precise, because things got quite hectic and my awareness was fading in and out.

5:30 AM. Entered the Labor and Delivery room.

6:00 AM. Took the first misoprostol tablet, got an IV line placed, and had pre-labor blood drawn. I was still allowed to eat at this point, so my husband and I ordered some omelets — the last real food I'd have that day.

7:00 AM. Met my prenatal OB. She asked if she could do a cervical check — I agreed without clarifying what exactly she had in mind, and she went ahead and did a membrane sweep. Extremely painful, and quite a bit of bleeding. She then recommended placing a Foley balloon catheter to further ripen the cervix and shorten the first stage. While I could still move freely, I rushed to the bathroom — my last self-powered trip to the toilet that day.

7:30 AM. Received a narcotic injection to reduce the pain of the balloon placement. Instantly felt knocked out.

8:00 AM. The original OB was called away for surgery, so a different OB came to place the Foley balloon. Possibly because of the narcotic, the experience wasn't as terrifying as the membrane sweep. After the balloon was in place, a tube was taped to my thigh. I was told I was at 2 to 3 centimeters dilation — still able to move around.

10:00 to 10:53 AM. Started a normal saline IV drip. Then, once the anesthesiologist was available, I got the epidural. I wasn't feeling many contractions at that point, so it went okay. They had me sit upright on the edge of the bed with my feet dangling, hugging a pillow. I felt incredibly cold and couldn't stop shaking.

12:00 PM. Pitocin drip started. I began feeling contractions — the top of my uterus would harden and press against my ribs, but there was no pain.

2:39 PM. Starving. I asked the nurse if I could have some orange juice. Told I could only have clear fluids — gave up. The nurse tried a gentle tug on the balloon, and it came right out. I was at 3 to 5 centimeters dilated.

2:45 PM. Before the nurse could even notify the OB, I felt a distinct pop followed by a big gush of fluid. The nurse confirmed my membranes had ruptured spontaneously.

3:12 PM. The epidural seemed to have a gap — my lower-left abdomen was in serious pain. I self-administered an extra dose. You're only allowed one every 20 minutes.

3:25 PM. OB arrived for another cervical check — 7 centimeters dilated, in active labor.

6:11 PM. OB checked again — fully dilated, baby at station plus one. Time to push.
